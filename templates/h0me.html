Journey to Meet Your SMART Goal

Start Realizing the Benefits of Tracking Your Expenses.

Logging into your bank’s website and scrolling through your bank statements once in a blue moon is no way to manage your personal finances. You need a better approach. The benefits of tracking your expenses as opposed to ignoring how much you spend and on what are potentially life changing. But as important as tracking your spending is, it’s really just the first step.

npbudget.com THE ULTIMATE BUDGET PLANNER is an online budget planner build to help users reach their SMART goals in a timely manner.



Benefits of Tracking Your Expenses

The benefits of tracking your expenses include finding and eliminating bad spending habits, preventing overspending, making sure you pay yourself first, and ensuring your spending reflects your priorities. You can also lower financial stress and spot fraudulent activity when you track your expenses.


Puts You Back in Control

It’s a terrible feeling not knowing where your money goes. You wonder how it came to be that you work hard, yet you don’t have enough cash to afford a cheeseburger until next payday. You have to take control. No one is going to stop you from spending all your money. Friends, family, and businesses directly and indirectly encourage it. You’re in charge of your financial decisions. The only one who can make sure you don’t wind up broke, unable to retire, and wondering how it all happened is you. Taking back control starts with tracking your expenses.

Show Your Bad Spending Habits

When you start tracking your expenses certain patterns, habits, and spending triggers you might not even be aware of emerge. You might unearth unused subscriptions or small daily expenses you can easily eliminate like that daily latte. Buying drinks, snacks, gum, and other stupid stuff. I was actually embarrassed enough to cut it out completely. It’s not a ton of cash, but it looks better going into my emergency fund rather than going toward Gatorade, beef jerky, and gummy bears. Tracking your spending helps you get rid of bad money habits and make more financially sound decisions.

Helps You Avoid Debt

If you have student loans, a car payment, and carry credit card debt, seeing how much you pay to creditors every month is illuminating. You can drill down even further by examining your credit card statements and determining how much of your monthly debt payments go toward interest and other fees. You’ll be inspired to focus on getting out of debt as quickly as possible. You will start saving money and avoiding debt at all cost so that you won't have to worry about it in the future. You’ll also be discouraged from taking on any new debt. This will lead you through a successful financial journey.


About

FinTech Focus 2021 ❤️ JPMorgan Chase Cohort


NP Budget was created because we recognized the importance for people to keep track of their daily savings and spendings. The website was built to mostly target college students. It is a perfect platform for these students to manage their finances while trying to meet their SMART goals.

FinTech Focus is a four-week immersive program designed for incoming college freshmen with an interest in finance, computer science and technology. Over the course of the program, we learned the principles of computer science, gain exposure to the financial services industry, and get hands-on experience developing real-world applications. We were able to master the core components of the Python development language. NP Budget is the website we (Maggie & Sen) created using HTML and CSS as part of our final project!

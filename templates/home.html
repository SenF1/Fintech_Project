Journey to Meet Your SMART Goal

Start Realizing the Benefits of Tracking Your Expenses.

Logging into your bank’s website and scrolling through your bank statements once in a blue moon is no way to manage your personal finances. You need a better approach. The benefits of tracking your expenses as opposed to ignoring how much you spend and on what are potentially life changing. But as important as tracking your spending is, it’s really just the first step.

npbudget.com THE ULTIMATE BUDGET PLANNER is an online budget planner build to help users reach their SMART goals in a timely manner.

Puts You Back in Control

It’s a terrible feeling not knowing where your money goes. You wonder how it came to be that you work hard, yet you don’t have enough cash to afford a cheeseburger until next payday. You feel lost and out of control when it comes to your personal finances. I’ve been there. And I don’t ever want to go back to that place. You have to take control. No one is going to stop you from spending all your money. Friends, family, and businesses directly and indirectly encourage it. You’re in charge of your financial decisions. The only one who can make sure you don’t wind up broke, unable to retire, and wondering how it all happened is you. Taking back control starts with tracking your expenses.

Identifies Budget Categories to Reevaluate

What percentage of your net income is spent on housing, food, and transportation? How much money do you save for a rainy day every month? What about your discretionary spending? The answers to these questions can easily be found by tracking your expenses. Once you know how much you spend and for what, you can see where you can cut or eliminate some spending. After seeing the numbers, you might decide reducing your housing costs, driving a more affordable car, or setting a food budget will make an immediate and lasting impact.

Shows You Your Bad Spending Habits

When you start tracking your expenses certain patterns, habits, and spending triggers you might not even be aware of emerge. You might unearth unused subscriptions or small daily expenses you can easily eliminate like that daily latte. For example, it wasn’t until I started tracking my expenses I realized I was spending over $40 a week in gas stations and convenience stores. Buying drinks, snacks, gum, and other stupid stuff. I was actually embarrassed enough to cut it out completely. It’s not a ton of cash, but it looks better going into my emergency fund rather than going toward Gatorade, beef jerky, and gummy bears. Tracking your spending helps you get rid of bad money habits and make more financially sound decisions.

Keeps You From Overspending

What you think you spend on certain things and what you actually spend could be miles apart. You’ll never know it if you’re not tracking spending. For example, Sara and I were spending hundreds more on food than we thought we were. It was shocking to see, but the numbers weren’t lying. We immediately went about figuring out ways to reduce our grocery spending and committing to doing so.

Helps You Avoid Debt

If you have student loans, a car payment, and carry credit card debt, seeing how much you pay to creditors every month is illuminating. You can drill down even further by examining your credit card statements and determining how much of your monthly debt payments go toward interest and other fees. You’ll be inspired to focus on getting out of debt as quickly as possible. You’ll also be discouraged from taking on any new debt.
